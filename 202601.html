Starting a Small Business in Australia

Published: 5 January 2026

Starting a small business can be exciting, but it also involves careful planning. Establishing a clear strategy from the beginning can help you save time, reduce stress, and make better decisions. A website is often the first place potential customers interact with your business, so building a clear and accessible online presence is important.

Tip: Begin by defining your brand and understanding who your customers are; this will guide many decisions down the line.

1. Define Your Brand

Your brand is more than a logo. It shapes how people perceive your business. Consider:

  • Mission and values
  • Target audience
  • Personality and tone
  • Visual style (colors, fonts)

2. Register Your Business Legally

Before trading, ensure your business complies with Australian regulations:

  • ABN: Needed for tax and official dealings. Register here
  • Business name: Required if your trading name differs from your personal name. Register here
  • Licences & permits: See ABLiS

3. Choose the Right Website Solution

Your website is where customers learn about your business. Ensure it is:

  • Fast and easy to navigate
  • Secure and reliable
  • Flexible for future updates
  • Accessible to all users

4. Focus on User Experience

A website that is easy to use keeps visitors engaged:

  • Clear, mobile-friendly navigation
  • Readable and simple content
  • Helpful calls-to-action

5. Prioritise SEO & Visibility

Optimising your website helps potential customers find you:

  • Use descriptive headings and content
  • Ensure fast page loading
  • Maintain clean, lightweight code

6. Plan for Ongoing Maintenance

Websites require regular updates to stay secure and functional:

  • Keep software and plugins up to date
  • Monitor website performance
  • Refresh content to stay relevant
